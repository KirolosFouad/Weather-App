winter

Winter is the coldest and darkest season of the year in polar and temperate climates. It occurs after autumn and before spring.

Summer

Summer is the hottest and brightest of the four temperate seasons, occurring after spring and before autumn.

Spring

Spring, also known as springtime, is one of the four temperate seasons, succeeding winter and preceding summer.

Autumn

Autumn, also known as fall,[1] is one of the four temperate seasons on Earth. Outside the tropics, autumn marks the transition from summer to winter.
